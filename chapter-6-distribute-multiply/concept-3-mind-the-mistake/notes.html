Chapter 6 · Concept 3

Mind the Mistake

Half of algebra marks are lost to a handful of repeated slips. Learn to spot them, explain what went wrong, and write the correct expression.

Key Concept The classic errors

1.
Forgetting the power. −3p × −5p = +15p², not −15p. Multiplying p by p gives p².
2.
Dropping the middle term. (5m + 6n)² is 25m² + 60mn + 36n², not 25m² + 36n².
3.
Sign slips. −3p(−5p + 2q) = 15p² − 6pq. Each term inside keeps its sign rule.
4.
Combining unlike terms. 5w² and 6w cannot be added; 2a²b and 3ab² are different terms.
5.
Order of operations. y + 2(y + 2) = y + 2y + 4 = 3y + 4 — multiply the bracket first, then add y.
Best habit: expand fully first, then collect like terms at the end. Most mistakes happen when steps are skipped or combined too early.
Prerequisite refresher: "Like terms" have exactly the same letters and powers. Only like terms can be added or subtracted into one term.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — (a + b)² ≠ a² + b². The missing 2ab is the single most common error.
Trap 2 — Negative × negative. (−3p)(−5p) is positive: +15p².
Trap 3 — Adding unlike terms. 5w² + 6w stays as it is; you cannot make 11w².
Trap 4 — Multiplying only some terms. In y + 2(y + 2), the 2 multiplies both y and 2 inside the bracket, but not the y outside.
Slip 5 — Check by substituting a number (e.g. put a = 2, b = 3) into both your answer and the original to confirm they match.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Spot and correct: −3p(−5p + 2q) = −3p + 5p − 2q.
Mistake:The −3p was not multiplied — it was just added to the terms.
Correct:−3p × (−5p) = 15p²; −3p × 2q = −6pq.
∴ 15p² − 6pq
Example B. Spot and correct: 2(x − 1) + 3(x + 4) = 5x + 3.
Mistake:The constants were added wrongly: −2 + 12 = 10, not 3.
Correct:2x − 2 + 3x + 12 = 5x + 10.
∴ 5x + 10
Example C. Spot and correct: (5m + 6n)² = 25m² + 36n².
Mistake:The middle term 2 × (5m) × (6n) = 60mn was left out.
Correct:(5m)² + 2(5m)(6n) + (6n)² = 25m² + 60mn + 36n².
∴ 25m² + 60mn + 36n²
Example D. Spot and correct: 2a³ + 3a³ + 6a²b + 6ab² = 5a³ + 12a²b².
Mistake:6a²b and 6ab² are not like terms, so they cannot be combined into 12a²b².
Correct:2a³ + 3a³ = 5a³; the other two stay separate.
∴ 5a³ + 6a²b + 6ab²
