Distributive Property & Expanding

The distributive property is the engine of algebra. Learn to multiply a bracket by a term, multiply two brackets, and tidy up by combining like terms.

Key Concept Multiplying out brackets

The rule: Multiplication distributes over addition — multiply the outside term by each term inside the bracket.
a(b + c) = ab + ac

Multiplying two brackets

Multiply every term in the first bracket by every term in the second, then add the results.

(a + m)(b + n) = ab + an + mb + mn

This is the master identity of the chapter. With m and n possibly negative, it covers every "expand the brackets" question.

Like terms

After expanding, add together like terms — terms with exactly the same letters and powers. 3a²b + 2a²b = 5a²b, but a²b and ab² cannot be combined.

Why it's useful: the distributive property even gives quick mental-maths tricks. 23 × 27 + 23 = 23(27 + 1) = 23 × 28.
Prerequisite refresher: "Expand" means remove the brackets by multiplying out. We usually drop the × sign: a(b+c) means a × (b + c), and 5a means 5 × a.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Multiply every term. a(b + c) = ab + ac, not ab + c. The outside term hits both inside terms.
Trap 2 — Signs. −3p(−5p + 2q) = +15p² − 6pq. A negative times a negative is positive; keep track of every sign.
Trap 3 — Two brackets = four products. (a + m)(b + n) gives four terms before you simplify. Don't multiply only the "firsts" and "lasts".
Trap 4 — Only like terms combine. a²b and ab² are different; they cannot be added into one term.
Slip 5 — Powers when multiplying. p × p = p², not 2p.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Expand 3a(a − b + 1).
Step 1:Multiply 3a by each term inside.
Step 2:3a × a = 3a²; 3a × (−b) = −3ab; 3a × 1 = 3a.
∴ 3a² − 3ab + 3a
Example B. Expand (3 + u)(v − 3).
Step 1:Multiply each term of the first bracket by each term of the second.
Step 2:3×v + 3×(−3) + u×v + u×(−3) = 3v − 9 + uv − 3u.
∴ uv − 3u + 3v − 9
Example C. Expand (10a + b)(10c + d).
Step 1:Four products: 10a×10c + 10a×d + b×10c + b×d.
Step 2:= 100ac + 10ad + 10bc + bd.
∴ 100ac + 10ad + 10bc + bd
Example D. Simplify 3a²b + 2a²b + 4ab².
Step 1:3a²b and 2a²b are like terms: 3a²b + 2a²b = 5a²b.
Step 2:4ab² has different letters/powers, so it stays separate.
∴ 5a²b + 4ab²
