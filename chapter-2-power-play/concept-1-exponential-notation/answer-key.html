Final answers with a one-line reason. For full methods, see the worked examples in the notes.

Section A Objective

Q1
(b) exponent. In 5⁴, 5 is the base and 4 is the exponent (power).
Q2
(a) 2³ × 3⁴. 648 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3.
Q3
16. Even power of a negative base is positive: (−2)⁴ = 16.
Q4
(b) −16. Without brackets, −2⁴ = −(2⁴) = −16.
Q5
True. Odd power of a negative base is negative: (−5)³ = −125.
Q6
(a) Both true, R explains A. Each square is positive (9 and 25), giving 225.
