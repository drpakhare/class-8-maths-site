Exponential Notation

A power is a short way to write repeated multiplication. Learn the words base and exponent, how to turn products into powers, and how to write a number as a product of prime powers.

Key Concept Base, exponent and value

The idea: nᵃ means n multiplied by itself a times. We call n the base and a the exponent (or power).
5⁴ = 5 × 5 × 5 × 5 = 625   |   base = 5, exponent = 4

Read 5⁴ as "5 raised to the power 4" or "5 to the power 4". Special names: a² is "squared", a³ is "cubed".

Writing a product as a power

If the same factor repeats, count how many times and write it as a power. Different factors get their own powers.

2 × 2 × 2 × a × a = 2³ × a² = 2³a²

Powers with negative bases — watch the sign

•
A negative base raised to an even power is positive: (−4)² = 16.
•
A negative base raised to an odd power is negative: (−4)³ = −64.

Prime factorisation in exponential form

Break the number into primes, then write each repeated prime as a power.

648 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 2³ × 3⁴
Prerequisite refresher: A "factor" is a number that divides exactly. "Prime factorisation" writes a number using only prime factors (2, 3, 5, 7, …).

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Power ≠ multiply by the exponent. 5⁴ = 625, not 5 × 4 = 20.
Trap 2 — Sign of a negative base. (−2)⁴ = +16 (even power), but (−2)⁵ = −32 (odd power). Count the power's parity.
Trap 3 — −2⁴ vs (−2)⁴. Without brackets, −2⁴ = −(2⁴) = −16. With brackets, (−2)⁴ = +16. Brackets matter.
Trap 4 — Counting the primes. In prime factorisation, count each prime carefully — one extra or missing factor changes the exponent.
Slip 5 — Notation. Write the exponent small and raised: 2³. "23" means twenty-three.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Express 2 × 2 × a × a × a in exponential form.
Step 1:Count each repeated factor: 2 appears twice, a appears three times.
Step 2:Write each as a power: 2² and a³.
∴ 2 × 2 × a × a × a = 2²a³
Example B. Express 648 as a product of prime powers.
Step 1:Divide by primes: 648 = 2 × 324 = 2 × 2 × 162 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 81.
Step 2:81 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3, so 648 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3.
Step 3:Write repeated primes as powers.
∴ 648 = 2³ × 3⁴
Example C. Find the value of (−3)² × (−5)².
Step 1:(−3)² = 9 (even power → positive).
Step 2:(−5)² = 25 (even power → positive).
Step 3:9 × 25 = 225.
∴ (−3)² × (−5)² = 225
Example D. Find the value of (−2)⁵ × (−10)⁶.
Step 1:(−2)⁵ = −32 (odd power → negative).
Step 2:(−10)⁶ = 1000000 (even power → positive).
Step 3:−32 × 1000000 = −32000000.
∴ (−2)⁵ × (−10)⁶ = −32000000
