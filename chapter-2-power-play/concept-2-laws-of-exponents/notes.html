Laws of Exponents

Five rules let you combine powers without expanding them. Master when to add, subtract, or multiply the exponents — this is the heart of the chapter.

Key Concept The five laws

In all of these the bases must be the same (for the first three) — that is the key condition.

1.
Product law — same base, ADD:   nᵃ × nᵇ = na+b.   e.g. 2³ × 2⁵ = 2⁸.
2.
Quotient law — same base, SUBTRACT:   nᵃ ÷ nᵇ = na−b.   e.g. 7⁹ ÷ 7⁴ = 7⁵.
3.
Power of a power — MULTIPLY:   (nᵃ)ᵇ = na×b.   e.g. (5²)³ = 5⁶.
4.
Power of a product:   nᵃ × mᵃ = (n × m)ᵃ.   e.g. 4³ × 5³ = 20³.
5.
Power of a quotient:   nᵃ ÷ mᵃ = (n ÷ m)ᵃ  (m ≠ 0).
Memory hook: multiplying powers → add; dividing powers → subtract; a power raised to a power → multiply.
Note: These laws also work for zero and negative exponents (next concept). Here we practise with positive exponents.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Adding when you should multiply. (5²)³ is power-of-a-power, so multiply: 5⁶. It is NOT 5²⁺³ = 5⁵.
Trap 2 — Multiplying the bases in the product law. 2³ × 2⁵ = 2⁸, NOT 4⁸. The base stays the same; only the exponents add.
Trap 3 — Using the laws with different bases. The add/subtract laws need the same base. 2³ × 3³ uses law 4: (2×3)³ = 6³, not 6⁶ or 5³.
Trap 4 — Direction of subtraction. nᵃ ÷ nᵇ = na−b (top minus bottom). Don't flip it.
Slip 5 — Leave it in exponential form unless asked for the value. 3⁴ × 3² = 3⁶ is a complete answer.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Simplify 3⁴ × 3² and write in exponential form.
Step 1:Same base 3, multiplying → use the product law (add exponents).
Step 2:3⁴ × 3² = 3⁴⁺² = 3⁶.
∴ 3⁴ × 3² = 3⁶
Example B. Simplify 6⁷ ÷ 6³.
Step 1:Same base 6, dividing → quotient law (subtract exponents).
Step 2:6⁷ ÷ 6³ = 6⁷⁻³ = 6⁴.
∴ 6⁷ ÷ 6³ = 6⁴
Example C. Simplify (5²)³.
Step 1:A power raised to a power → multiply the exponents.
Step 2:(5²)³ = 5²ˣ³ = 5⁶.
∴ (5²)³ = 5⁶
Example D. Write 8⁶ as a power of a power in two different ways.
Idea:Split the exponent 6 into two factors in different ways: 6 = 2 × 3 = 3 × 2.
Way 1:8⁶ = (8²)³.
Way 2:8⁶ = (8³)².
∴ 8⁶ = (8²)³ = (8³)²
Example E. Simplify 2⁵ × 2³ ÷ 2⁴ and find its value.
Step 1:Same base — add then subtract exponents: 5 + 3 − 4 = 4.
Step 2:So the expression = 2⁴ = 16.
∴ 2⁵ × 2³ ÷ 2⁴ = 2⁴ = 16
