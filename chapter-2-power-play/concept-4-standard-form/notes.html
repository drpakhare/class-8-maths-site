Powers of 10 & Standard Form

Standard (scientific) form is a tidy way to write very large or very small numbers using powers of 10 — so you never miscount zeros.

Key Concept What is standard form?

The idea: Every number can be written as x × 10ʸ, where x is at least 1 and less than 10 (so exactly one non-zero digit before the decimal point) and y is an integer.
5900 = 5.9 × 10³   |   0.00037 = 3.7 × 10⁻⁴

How to write a number in standard form

1.
Put the decimal point just after the first non-zero digit to get x.
2.
Count how many places the point moved. That count is the power y.
3.
Large number (point moved left) → positive power. Small decimal (point moved right) → negative power.

Expanded form with powers of 10

47561 = (4 × 10⁴) + (7 × 10³) + (5 × 10²) + (6 × 10¹) + (1 × 10⁰)

Comparing numbers in standard form

First compare the power of 10; the bigger power wins. If the powers are equal, compare the coefficients x.

Prerequisite refresher: 10¹ = 10, 10² = 100, 10³ = 1000 …; and 10⁻¹ = 0.1, 10⁻² = 0.01, 10⁻³ = 0.001 …

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — x out of range. In standard form x must be ≥ 1 and < 10. So 59 × 10² is NOT standard form; 5.9 × 10³ is.
Trap 2 — Wrong sign on the power. Big numbers get a positive power; small decimals get a negative power. 0.0004 = 4 × 10⁻⁴, not 4 × 10⁴.
Trap 3 — Miscounting places. Count the decimal-point moves carefully — that count is the exponent.
Trap 4 — Comparing only the front digits. 9.35 × 10⁶ is smaller than 6.27 × 10⁷, because 10⁶ < 10⁷. Check the power first.
Slip 5 — Tidy the product. After multiplying, e.g. 13.12 × 10⁵, rewrite so x < 10: 1.312 × 10⁶.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Write 47561 in expanded form using powers of 10.
Step 1:Give each digit its place value as a power of 10.
Step 2:47561 = (4 × 10⁴) + (7 × 10³) + (5 × 10²) + (6 × 10¹) + (1 × 10⁰).
∴ 47561 = (4 × 10⁴) + (7 × 10³) + (5 × 10²) + (6 × 10¹) + (1 × 10⁰)
Example B. Write 39,00,00,000 in standard form.
Step 1:The number is 390000000. Put the point after the first digit: 3.9.
Step 2:The point moved 8 places to the left → power = 8 (positive, large number).
∴ 39,00,00,000 = 3.9 × 10⁸
Example C. Write 0.000005678 in standard form.
Step 1:First non-zero digit is 5; write 5.678.
Step 2:The point moved 6 places to the right → power = −6 (small number).
∴ 0.000005678 = 5.678 × 10⁻⁶
Example D. Express 3.2 × 10⁶ × 4.1 × 10⁻¹ in standard form.
Step 1:Multiply the coefficients: 3.2 × 4.1 = 13.12.
Step 2:Add the powers of 10: 10⁶ × 10⁻¹ = 10⁵. So we have 13.12 × 10⁵.
Step 3:Tidy so x < 10: 13.12 × 10⁵ = 1.312 × 10⁶.
∴ = 1.312 × 10⁶
Example E. Which is larger: 1.4335 × 10¹² or 1.439 × 10¹²?
Step 1:The powers are equal (both 10¹²), so compare the coefficients.
Step 2:1.439 > 1.4335.
∴ 1.439 × 10¹² is larger
