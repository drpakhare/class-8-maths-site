Sharing & Unit Conversions

Use ratios to share a quantity fairly, and convert between units of length, area, volume and temperature.

Key Concept Sharing a quantity in a ratio

The idea: To divide a quantity x in the ratio m : n, split it into (m + n) equal groups. Each group is x ÷ (m + n).
First part = m ⁄ (m + n) × x   |   Second part = n ⁄ (m + n) × x

Example: share 42 in the ratio 4 : 3. Groups = 4 + 3 = 7, each group = 42 ÷ 7 = 6, so the parts are 4 × 6 = 24 and 3 × 6 = 18.

Handy unit conversions

QuantityConversion
Length1 metre = 3.281 feet
Area1 hectare = 10,000 m²;   1 acre = 43,560 ft²
Volume1 litre = 1000 mL;   1 mL = 1 cm³
Temperature°F = (9⁄5 × °C) + 32;   °C = 5⁄9 × (°F − 32)
Check yourself: after sharing, the parts should add back to the whole (24 + 18 = 42).
Prerequisite refresher: m ⁄ (m + n) means "m out of every (m + n) parts". The two fractions add to 1.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Use the total number of parts. For ratio 4 : 3, divide by 7 (= 4 + 3), not by 4 or by 3.
Trap 2 — Match part to ratio number. The 4-share gets 4 groups, the 3-share gets 3 groups.
Trap 3 — Convert before comparing. A weight-to-price ratio of 1 kg : ₹800 is really 1000 g : 800, depending on the unit you fix.
Trap 4 — Temperature isn't a simple ratio. Use the formula °F = 9⁄5 × °C + 32; you cannot just multiply by a single factor.
Slip 5 — Add the parts back to check they total the original quantity.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Share 42 counters in the ratio 4 : 3.
Step 1:Total parts = 4 + 3 = 7; each part = 42 ÷ 7 = 6.
Step 2:Shares = 4 × 6 = 24 and 3 × 6 = 18.
∴ 24 and 18
Example B. A mortar mix of 40 kg has sand : cement = 3 : 1. Find each weight.
Step 1:Total parts = 3 + 1 = 4; each part = 40 ÷ 4 = 10 kg.
Step 2:Sand = 3 × 10 = 30 kg; cement = 1 × 10 = 10 kg.
∴ 30 kg sand, 10 kg cement
Example C. Convert 25 °C to Fahrenheit.
Step 1:°F = (9⁄5 × °C) + 32 = (9⁄5 × 25) + 32.
Step 2:= 45 + 32 = 77.
∴ 25 °C = 77 °F
Example D. The masses of equal volumes of gold and water are in the ratio 37 : 2. If 1 litre of water is 1 kg, find the mass of 1 litre of gold.
Step 1:gold : water = 37 : 2 and water = 1 kg, so 1 kg matches the "2" part.
Step 2:One part = 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5 kg, so gold = 37 × 0.5 = 18.5 kg.
∴ 18.5 kg
