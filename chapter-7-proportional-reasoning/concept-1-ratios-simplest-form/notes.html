Ratios & Simplest Form

A ratio compares two quantities. Learn how to write one, reduce it to simplest form, and decide when two figures (or recipes, or mixtures) are proportional.

Key Concept What is a ratio?

The idea: A ratio a : b compares two quantities — for every a units of the first there are b units of the second. The numbers a and b are called the terms of the ratio.
Width : height of an image = 60 : 40

Simplest form

Divide both terms by their highest common factor (HCF). The ratio's meaning doesn't change.

72 : 96 = (72 ÷ 24) : (96 ÷ 24) = 3 : 4

Proportional (similar) quantities

Two ratios are proportional when they are equal in their simplest forms. Rectangles are similar when their width : height ratios match.

•
60 : 40 and 30 : 20 are both 3 : 2 in simplest form, so those rectangles are similar.
•
A square 60 : 60 is 1 : 1 — not 3 : 2 — so it looks different (distorted).
Watch the units: compare like with like. 1 kg : 800 g is really 1000 g : 800 g = 5 : 4, not 1 : 800.
Prerequisite refresher: "HCF" is the largest number that divides both terms. Reducing a ratio is like reducing a fraction.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Mixed units. Convert both terms to the same unit before writing a ratio (1 m : 50 cm = 100 cm : 50 cm = 2 : 1).
Trap 2 — Order matters. 3 : 2 is not the same as 2 : 3. Write the terms in the order asked.
Trap 3 — Reduce fully. 24 : 18 is not simplest; divide by 6 to get 4 : 3.
Trap 4 — Adding to terms breaks proportion. Adding the same number to both terms of a ratio usually changes it (3 : 30 is 1 : 10, but 12 : 39 is 4 : 13).
Slip 5 — A ratio has no units. After simplifying, write just the numbers (4 : 3), not "4 cm : 3 cm".

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Write 600 : 900 in its simplest form.
Step 1:HCF of 600 and 900 is 300.
Step 2:600 ÷ 300 = 2; 900 ÷ 300 = 3.
∴ 2 : 3
Example B. Are the ratios 3 : 4 and 72 : 96 proportional?
Step 1:3 : 4 is already simplest.
Step 2:72 : 96 = 3 : 4 (divide both by 24).
Step 3:Both simplest forms are 3 : 4, so they are proportional.
∴ Yes, proportional
Example C. A rectangle is 60 mm wide and 40 mm high. Is it similar to a 30 mm × 20 mm rectangle?
Step 1:60 : 40 = 3 : 2; 30 : 20 = 3 : 2.
Step 2:The width : height ratios are equal.
∴ Yes, the rectangles are similar
Example D. Anagh mixes 600 mL orange juice with 900 mL apple juice. Write the ratio in simplest form.
Step 1:Orange : apple = 600 : 900.
Step 2:Divide both by 300: 2 : 3.
∴ 2 : 3
