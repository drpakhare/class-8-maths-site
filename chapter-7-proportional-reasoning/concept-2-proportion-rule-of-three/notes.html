Proportion & the Rule of Three

When two ratios are equal we have a proportion. Cross multiplication turns it into a quick way to find an unknown — the ancient Rule of Three.

Key Concept Proportion & cross multiplication

The idea: a : b :: c : d means the two ratios are equal. Then the cross products are equal: a × d = b × c.
a : b :: c : d  ⟺  ad = bc

The Rule of Three (Trairāśika)

When three quantities of a proportion are known and one is unknown, set up a : b :: c : x and cross-multiply to find x.

6 : 10 :: 18 : x  →  6 × x = 10 × 18  →  x = 180 ÷ 6 = 30

Keep the units consistent

Both ratios must use the same units. A car going 90 km in 150 minutes — to find the distance in 4 hours, first change 4 hours to 240 minutes.

150 : 90 :: 240 : x  →  150x = 90 × 240  →  x = 144 km
Quick proportion check: to test whether a : b :: c : d, just compare the cross products ad and bc. If they're equal, it is a true proportion.
Prerequisite refresher: "Cross products" are the diagonal products a × d and b × c of a : b :: c : d.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Mismatched units. Convert everything to one unit before forming the proportion (minutes with minutes, grams with grams).
Trap 2 — Cross the right terms. For a : b :: c : d, multiply a × d and b × c — the diagonals, not a × c.
Trap 3 — Set up the order carefully. Keep "first quantity" with "first quantity" on both sides of the ::.
Trap 4 — Not everything is a proportion. Faster speed → less time (inverse), so a simple Rule of Three doesn't apply when one goes up as the other goes down.
Slip 5 — State the unit of the answer (km, kg, spoons…), not just the number.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Is 4 : 7 :: 12 : 21 a true proportion?
Step 1:Cross products: 4 × 21 = 84 and 7 × 12 = 84.
Step 2:They are equal, so the proportion is true.
∴ Yes, it is a true proportion
Example B. Solve for x: 6 : 10 :: 18 : x.
Step 1:Cross multiply: 6 × x = 10 × 18 = 180.
Step 2:x = 180 ÷ 6 = 30.
∴ x = 30
Example C. A car travels 90 km in 150 minutes. How far will it go in 4 hours at the same speed?
Step 1:Make units match: 4 hours = 240 minutes.
Step 2:150 : 90 :: 240 : x, so 150x = 90 × 240 = 21600.
Step 3:x = 21600 ÷ 150 = 144.
∴ 144 km
Example D. For 120 students a cook makes 15 kg of rice. How much rice for 80 students?
Step 1:120 : 15 :: 80 : x.
Step 2:120x = 15 × 80 = 1200, so x = 10.
∴ 10 kg
