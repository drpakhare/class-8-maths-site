Section B Short answer

Q7
6 zeros. Trailing zeros double on squaring (3 → 6).
Q8
All four are not perfect squares. 1057 ends in 7; 23453 ends in 3; 7928 ends in 8 (forbidden last digits); 222000 has 3 trailing zeros — an odd number of zeros.
Q9
100, 121, 144. These are 10², 11², 12². (81 is below 90; 169 is above 150.)

Section C Long answer

Q10
√36 = 6. 36−1−3−5−7−9−11 reaches 0 after 6 subtractions ⇒ perfect square, root = 6.
Q11
36² = 1296. 36² = 35² + (35 + 36) = 1225 + 71.
Q12
Side = 13 m. 13² = 169; its units digit 9 is an allowed square-ending, which confirms 169 can be a perfect square.
