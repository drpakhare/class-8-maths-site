Divisibility & Digital Roots

Quick tests to check divisibility without dividing, and the digital-root shortcut that powers many of them.

Key Concept The divisibility tests

Divisible byQuick test
2last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, 8)
5last digit is 0 or 5
10last digit is 0
4the last two digits form a number divisible by 4
8the last three digits form a number divisible by 8
3the sum of the digits is divisible by 3
9the sum of the digits is divisible by 9
6divisible by both 2 and 3
11the alternating sum of digits (from the units digit) is 0 or a multiple of 11

The digital root

Keep adding the digits of a number until a single digit remains — that is the digital root. It equals the remainder when the number is divided by 9 (and a multiple of 9 has digital root 9).

7309 → 7 + 3 + 0 + 9 = 19 → 1 + 9 = 10 → 1 + 0 = 1.   Digital root = 1
Why digit sums work for 9: every place value (10, 100, 1000…) is one more than a multiple of 9, so each digit contributes itself to the remainder. That's why the digit sum tells you the remainder on division by 9 (and the same idea, with 3, works for 3).
Prerequisite refresher: "Alternating sum" means +, −, +, − the digits. For 11, start the + at the units digit.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — 9 is not about the last digit. Both 99 and 109 end in 9, but only 99 is divisible by 9. Use the digit sum.
Trap 2 — Divisible by 4 and 6 ≠ divisible by 24. Check 24 by testing 3 and 8 (its co-prime factors), not 4 and 6.
Trap 3 — The 11 test. A result of 0 also passes — 0 is a multiple of 11.
Trap 4 — Digit sum gives the remainder. If the digit sum of a number is 30, it is divisible by 3 (30 is) but not by 9 (30 isn't).
Slip 5 — For 6, both tests must pass — divisible by 2 AND by 3.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. Is 405 divisible by 9?
Step 1:Add the digits: 4 + 0 + 5 = 9.
Step 2:9 is divisible by 9, so 405 is divisible by 9.
∴ Yes, 405 is divisible by 9
Example B. Find the value(s) of z so that 31z5 is a multiple of 9.
Step 1:Digit sum = 3 + 1 + z + 5 = 9 + z.
Step 2:9 + z must be a multiple of 9, so z = 0 or z = 9.
∴ z = 0 or 9
Example C. Check whether 406839 is divisible by 3, 9 and 11.
Step 1:Digit sum = 4 + 0 + 6 + 8 + 3 + 9 = 30. Divisible by 3 (yes), by 9 (no, 30 isn't).
Step 2:Alternating sum from units: 9 − 3 + 8 − 6 + 0 − 4 = 4, not a multiple of 11.
∴ Divisible by 3 only (not by 9 or 11)
Example D. Find the digital root of 7309.
Step 1:7 + 3 + 0 + 9 = 19.
Step 2:1 + 9 = 10, then 1 + 0 = 1.
∴ Digital root = 1
