Chapter 4 · Concept 1

Angles in Quadrilaterals

Every quadrilateral's angles add up to 360°. Learn why, how to find a missing angle, and the polygon angle facts (interior, exterior, and number of diagonals) that the exemplar tests.

Key Concept The angle sum is 360°

The rule: The four interior angles of any quadrilateral add up to 360°.
A diagonal cuts the quadrilateral into two triangles.

Why 360°? Draw one diagonal. It splits the quadrilateral into two triangles. Each triangle's angles add to 180°, so together: 180° + 180° = 360°.

∠A + ∠B + ∠C + ∠D = 360°

Polygon angle facts (used by the exemplar)

A polygon with n sides splits into (n − 2) triangles. A pentagon → 3.
•
Sum of interior angles of an n-sided polygon = (n − 2) × 180°. (Quadrilateral: (4−2)×180 = 360°.)
•
Sum of exterior angles of any polygon = 360° (one at each vertex).
•
Regular polygon: each exterior angle = 360° ⁄ n, and each interior angle = 180° − (360° ⁄ n).
•
Number of diagonals of an n-sided polygon = n(n − 3) ⁄ 2. (Quadrilateral: 4×1⁄2 = 2.)
Prerequisite refresher: "Interior angle" is the angle inside a corner. An "exterior angle" is the turn you make at a corner, and an interior + its exterior = 180°.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Using 180° for a quadrilateral. 180° is for a triangle. A quadrilateral is 360°.
Trap 2 — Ratio problems. If angles are in ratio 1:2:3:4, the parts add to 10, and 10 parts = 360°, so one part = 36°. Don't forget to multiply each ratio number by 36.
Trap 3 — Exterior-angle formula. For a regular polygon, number of sides = 360° ÷ (one exterior angle). Divide into 360, don't multiply.
Trap 4 — Diagonals formula. It is n(n−3)⁄2, not n(n−1)⁄2. A quadrilateral has 2 diagonals, not 6.
Slip 5 — Units. Always write the degree symbol (°) on angle answers.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. In quadrilateral ABCD, ∠A = 100°, ∠B = 80°, ∠C = 70°. Find ∠D.
Step 1:All four angles add to 360°: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C + ∠D = 360°.
Step 2:100° + 80° + 70° + ∠D = 360°, so 250° + ∠D = 360°.
Step 3:∠D = 360° − 250° = 110°.
∴ ∠D = 110°
Example B. The angles of a quadrilateral are in the ratio 1 : 2 : 3 : 4. Find each angle.
Step 1:Let the angles be 1x, 2x, 3x, 4x. Their sum is 360°.
Step 2:1x + 2x + 3x + 4x = 10x = 360°, so x = 36°.
Step 3:Angles = 36°, 72°, 108°, 144°.
∴ 36°, 72°, 108°, 144°
Example C. Each exterior angle of a regular polygon is 45°. How many sides has it?
Step 1:Sum of exterior angles = 360°, and they are all equal.
Step 2:Number of sides = 360° ÷ 45° = 8.
∴ 8 sides (an octagon)
Example D. How many diagonals does a hexagon (6 sides) have?
Step 1:Use the formula n(n − 3) ⁄ 2 with n = 6.
Step 2:6 × (6 − 3) ⁄ 2 = 6 × 3 ⁄ 2 = 9.
∴ 9 diagonals
