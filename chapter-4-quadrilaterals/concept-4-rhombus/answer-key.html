Rhombus — Answers

Final answers with a one-line reason. For full methods, see the worked examples in the notes.

Section A Objective

Q1
(c) Rhombus. All four sides equal; a square is a special rhombus.
Q2
(b) Rhombus. Diagonals bisecting at right angles ⇒ rhombus.
Q3
90. Rhombus diagonals are perpendicular.
Q4
(a) 50°. Opposite angles are equal.
Q5
False. They are perpendicular but generally unequal (equal only in a square).
Q6
(a) Both true, R explains A. Equal sides make a square a rhombus.

Section B Short answer

Q7
5 cm. Half-diagonals 3 and 4: √(3² + 4²) = 5.
Q8
70°, 110°, 70°. Opposite equal (110°); adjacent supplementary (70°).
Q9
x = 90°. Diagonals of a rhombus intersect at right angles.

Section C Long answer

Q10
60°, 120°, 60°, 120°. The diagonal = side makes an equilateral triangle (all 60°); adjacent angle = 120°.
Q11
13 cm. Half-diagonals 5 and 12: √(5² + 12²) = 13.
Q12
A square. Four equal sides make it a rhombus; one 90° angle forces all angles to 90°, so it is a square.
