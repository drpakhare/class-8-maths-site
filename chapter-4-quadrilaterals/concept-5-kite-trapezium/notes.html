Kite, Trapezium & the Family

Two more quadrilaterals — the kite and the trapezium — and a Venn diagram showing how every quadrilateral type fits together.

Key Concept The kite

Kite ABCD: AB = BC and CD = DA.
•
A kite has two pairs of adjacent (neighbouring) sides equal: AB = BC and CD = DA.
•
One pair of opposite angles are equal (the angles between the unequal sides).
•
The diagonals meet at right angles; the main diagonal bisects the other and bisects the two corner angles it passes through.

Key Concept The trapezium

Trapezium PQRS with PQ ∥ SR (shown by arrows).
•
A trapezium has at least one pair of parallel opposite sides.
•
The angles on the same leg (between the parallel sides) are supplementary: ∠P + ∠S = 180° and ∠Q + ∠R = 180°.
•
Isosceles trapezium: the non-parallel sides are equal, the base angles are equal, and the diagonals are equal.

Key Concept The quadrilateral family

Square = rectangle AND rhombus. Every rectangle, rhombus, and square is a parallelogram, and (inclusive view) every parallelogram is a trapezium.
•
A square sits in the overlap — it is both a rectangle and a rhombus.
•
A kite is separate, but a kite that is also a parallelogram is a rhombus.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Kite ≠ rhombus. A kite has two pairs of adjacent equal sides; a rhombus has all four equal. Not every kite is a rhombus.
Trap 2 — Trapezium angles. The supplementary pairs are the two angles on the same leg (between the parallel sides), not any two angles.
Trap 3 — Only one pair equal in a kite. Only one pair of opposite angles is equal, not both.
Trap 4 — Isosceles trapezium. "Isosceles" refers to the equal non-parallel sides (the legs), which give equal base angles.
Slip 5 — Name the figure precisely (e.g. "isosceles trapezium") to earn full marks.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. In trapezium PQRS, PQ ∥ SR. If ∠P = 120° and ∠Q = 100°, find ∠R and ∠S.
Step 1:∠P and ∠S are on the same leg PS, so they are supplementary: ∠S = 180° − 120° = 60°.
Step 2:∠Q and ∠R are on leg QR: ∠R = 180° − 100° = 80°.
∴ ∠R = 80°, ∠S = 60°
Example B. In kite ABCD (AB = BC, CD = DA), ∠B = 100° and ∠D = 40°. Find ∠A and ∠C.
Step 1:All angles add to 360°: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C + ∠D = 360°.
Step 2:In this kite ∠A = ∠C (the equal pair), so ∠A + ∠C = 360° − 100° − 40° = 220°.
Step 3:∠A = ∠C = 220° ÷ 2 = 110°.
∴ ∠A = ∠C = 110°
Example C. Which quadrilateral is both a rectangle and a rhombus?
Step 1:A rectangle has four right angles; a rhombus has four equal sides.
Step 2:A figure with both is a square.
∴ A square
