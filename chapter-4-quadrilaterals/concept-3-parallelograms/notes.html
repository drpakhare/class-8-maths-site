Parallelograms

A parallelogram has both pairs of opposite sides parallel. From that one fact flow all its angle and diagonal properties — the source of many exam questions.

Key Concept The four properties

Parallelogram ABCD; diagonals meet at O.
1.
Opposite sides are equal (and parallel). AB = DC and AD = BC.
2.
Opposite angles are equal. ∠A = ∠C and ∠B = ∠D.
3.
Adjacent angles are supplementary (add to 180°). ∠A + ∠B = 180°.
4.
The diagonals bisect each other (so OA = OC and OB = OD). They are not equal in general.
Why the angles work: Because the opposite sides are parallel, a side acts as a transversal — the co-interior angles on the same side add to 180°, which makes adjacent angles supplementary and opposite angles equal.
Prerequisite refresher: "Supplementary" = two angles that add to 180°. "Adjacent angles" share a side.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Opposite vs adjacent. Opposite angles are equal; adjacent angles are supplementary (add to 180°). Mixing these is the most common error.
Trap 2 — Diagonals are not equal. In a parallelogram the diagonals bisect each other but are generally unequal (equal only in a rectangle).
Trap 3 — Ratio of adjacent angles. If ∠A : ∠B = 2 : 3 and they are adjacent, then 2x + 3x = 180°, not 360°.
Trap 4 — "Diagonals bisect each other ⇒ parallelogram." This is true and is a useful test, but bisecting at right angles makes it a rhombus.
Slip 5 — Justify with the property name ("opposite angles of a parallelogram are equal") to earn full marks.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. In parallelogram ABCD, ∠A : ∠B = 2 : 3. Find ∠A and ∠B.
Step 1:∠A and ∠B are adjacent, so they are supplementary: ∠A + ∠B = 180°.
Step 2:Let them be 2x and 3x. Then 2x + 3x = 180°, so 5x = 180°, x = 36°.
Step 3:∠A = 2 × 36 = 72°, ∠B = 3 × 36 = 108°.
∴ ∠A = 72°, ∠B = 108°
Example B. One angle of a parallelogram is three times its adjacent angle. Find all four angles.
Step 1:Let the smaller angle be x; the other is 3x. Adjacent angles are supplementary.
Step 2:x + 3x = 180°, so 4x = 180°, x = 45°.
Step 3:The angles are 45° and 135°, and opposite angles are equal.
∴ 45°, 135°, 45°, 135°
Example C. In parallelogram ABCD, ∠A = 70°. Find ∠B, ∠C and ∠D.
Step 1:Opposite angles are equal: ∠C = ∠A = 70°.
Step 2:Adjacent angles are supplementary: ∠B = 180° − 70° = 110°.
Step 3:∠D = ∠B = 110° (opposite angles).
∴ ∠B = 110°, ∠C = 70°, ∠D = 110°
