Parallelograms — Answers

Final answers with a one-line reason. For full methods, see the worked examples in the notes.

Section A Objective

Q1
(c) equal. Opposite angles of a parallelogram are equal.
Q2
(b) parallelogram. Diagonals bisecting each other ⇒ parallelogram.
Q3
115. Adjacent angles are supplementary: 180 − 65 = 115.
Q4
(b) Diagonals equal. Parallelogram diagonals bisect each other but are generally unequal.
Q5
True. A standard parallelogram property.
Q6
(a) Both true, R explains A. The converse property is exactly why the figure is a parallelogram.

Section B Short answer

Q7
∠A = 72°, ∠B = 108°. 2x + 3x = 180 ⇒ x = 36.
Q8
y = 105°, z = 75°. y is adjacent to 75° (supplementary); z is opposite to 75° (equal).
Q9
PR = 8 cm, QS = 6 cm. Diagonals bisect each other, so PR = 2 × PO and QS = 2 × QO.

Section C Long answer

Q10
45°, 135°, 45°, 135°. x + 3x = 180 ⇒ x = 45; opposite angles equal.
Q11
∠B = 100°, ∠C = 80°, ∠D = 100°. ∠C = ∠A (opposite); ∠B = 180 − 80 (adjacent); ∠D = ∠B (opposite).
Q12
Fourth angle = 110°; not a parallelogram. 360 − (90 + 75 + 85) = 110; opposite angles (90 & 85, 75 & 110) are not equal, so it cannot be a parallelogram.
