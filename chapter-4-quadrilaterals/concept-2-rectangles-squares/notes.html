Rectangles & Squares

Both have four right angles. The key exam ideas are their diagonals — when they are equal, when they bisect each other, and when they meet at right angles.

Key Concept Properties & diagonals

Both diagonals are drawn dashed. In a square they also cross at 90°.

Rectangle

•
All four angles are 90°; opposite sides are equal and parallel.
•
The two diagonals are equal in length.
•
The diagonals bisect each other (cut each other in half).
•
Diagonal length (sides l and b) = √(l² + b²).

Square

•
All four sides equal and all four angles 90° (a square is a special rectangle).
•
Diagonals are equal, bisect each other, and meet at 90°; they also bisect the corner angles.
•
Diagonal length (side a) = a√2.
Prerequisite refresher: "Bisect" means cut exactly in half. "Diagonal" joins two opposite corners.

Common Traps Where marks slip away

Trap 1 — Rectangle diagonals aren't perpendicular. They are equal and bisect each other, but they only meet at 90° in a square.
Trap 2 — "Equal diagonals" setups. If two diagonal expressions are given, set them equal (rectangle diagonals are equal) and solve.
Trap 3 — Diagonal formula. Use √(l² + b²); don't just add l + b.
Trap 4 — Square is a rectangle and a rhombus. Every square is a rectangle (and a rhombus), but not every rectangle is a square.
Slip 5 — Leave √2 surd or round. a√2 is exact; if a number is wanted, use √2 ≈ 1.41.

Worked Examples Model answers — write them like this

Example A. The diagonals of a rectangle are (2x + 1) and (3x − 1). Find x and the diagonal length.
Step 1:In a rectangle the diagonals are equal: 2x + 1 = 3x − 1.
Step 2:1 + 1 = 3x − 2x, so x = 2.
Step 3:Diagonal = 2(2) + 1 = 5.
∴ x = 2, diagonal = 5
Example B. Find the length of the diagonal of a square whose side is 7 cm.
Step 1:Diagonal of a square = side × √2.
Step 2:= 7√2 ≈ 7 × 1.41 = 9.9 cm.
∴ Diagonal = 7√2 ≈ 9.9 cm
Example C. A rectangle has sides 6 cm and 8 cm. Find its diagonal.
Step 1:Diagonal = √(l² + b²) = √(6² + 8²).
Step 2:= √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 cm.
∴ Diagonal = 10 cm
